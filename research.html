Research

My research quantifies how irrigation and water scarcity reshape freshwater resources and food systems, using large-scale datasets, remote sensing, and geospatial analytics. At the University of Delaware, I lead the development of open datasets and analyses to support sustainable agriculture and resilient food–water systems. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

1) Develop and leverage large-scale environmental datasets

I build open, reusable datasets that enable global-to-local sustainability analysis. This includes the development of MIRCA-OS (monthly irrigated and rainfed cropped areas) and high-resolution irrigated area mapping using integrated remote sensing and machine learning. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

  • MIRCA-OS: global open-source monthly irrigated/rainfed crop areas :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • High-resolution (30m) global irrigated area dataset :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Co-authored data infrastructure efforts (e.g., HarvestStat) :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

2) Evolution of global & local water scarcity and impacts on food

I conduct global and local risk assessments of rising water scarcity on food production and supply, and analyze socioeconomic and environmental drivers of persistent economic water scarcity in developing regions. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

  • Risk assessment: water scarcity impacts on production & supply :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}
  • Drivers of persistent economic water scarcity :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}

3) Competition for water between sectors

My work examines trade-offs in water allocation and how scarcity pressures agriculture alongside urban, industrial, and environmental demands. I use spatial analytics and coupled data to help identify regions where sector competition may intensify and where policy can improve resilience.
