Research

My research integrates remote sensing, hydrological & crop–water modeling, and geospatial data science to understand how irrigation, climate stress, and socioeconomic constraints reshape water scarcity and food-system resilience. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Three future research themes

The descriptions below are adapted from my research statement. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

1) Fine-scale irrigation dynamics, water scarcity, and hydrologic sustainability

I develop and use AI-enabled Earth observation products to map irrigation intensity and timing, then integrate these data with hydrological and crop models to quantify consumptive water use, irrigation efficiency, and groundwater recharge deficits under climate and management scenarios. This links irrigation dynamics to basin-scale water balance and helps identify when and where irrigation shifts from sustainable use to hydrologic stress. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
